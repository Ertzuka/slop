The recent war involving Iran (2025–2026) is a major conflict in the Middle East primarily involving Iran, Israel, and the United States. It grew out of years of tension over Iran’s nuclear program, regional influence, and conflicts with Israel and Western allies.

Below is a concise but extensive overview in a similar structured format.

1. Background of the Conflict

The roots of the war go back decades.

Major causes include:

Iran’s nuclear program, which Israel and the U.S. fear could lead to nuclear weapons.

Long-standing hostility between Iran and Israel.

Iran’s support for regional armed groups opposed to Israel.

U.S. sanctions and diplomatic breakdown after the collapse of the 2015 nuclear agreement.

Tensions escalated through cyberattacks, covert operations, and proxy wars across the Middle East.

2. Early Escalations (2024–2025)

Direct military confrontation began to increase around 2024.

Key events included:

April 2024: Israel bombed an Iranian diplomatic facility in Syria, killing Iranian officials.

2024–2025: Missile exchanges and covert operations between Iran and Israel.

June 2025: Israel launched major strikes on Iranian nuclear facilities and military sites.

Iran retaliated with missile attacks, including strikes on a U.S. air base in Qatar.

A temporary ceasefire was reached in June 2025 but tensions remained high.

3. Major Escalation in 2026

A new and much larger war began 28 February 2026.

The United States and Israel launched coordinated attacks on Iranian military and nuclear infrastructure.

The operations targeted missile bases, nuclear facilities, and government leadership.

Iran responded with:

large-scale missile and drone attacks

strikes against U.S. bases in the region

attacks against Israeli targets.

The fighting quickly spread across the region.

4. Assassination of Iran’s Supreme Leader

One of the most dramatic events occurred early in the war.

On 28 February 2026, Ali Khamenei, the Supreme Leader of Iran, was killed in an airstrike near Tehran as part of the Israeli-U.S. operation.

This strike targeted Iran’s top leadership and caused major political upheaval inside the country.

Iran created an interim leadership council and declared national mourning.

5. Military Operations

The war has involved several types of military actions:

Airstrikes

Thousands of targets in Iran have reportedly been struck, including:

missile facilities

military bases

nuclear infrastructure

government buildings.

Missile and Drone Attacks

Iran has fired large numbers of missiles and drones at Israeli territory and U.S. bases.

Naval Warfare

There have also been attacks on ships and naval assets in regional waters.

6. Humanitarian Impact

The conflict has had significant civilian consequences.

Reports indicate:

over 1,000 civilian deaths in Iran during the conflict.

missile strikes on cities and infrastructure.

displacement of civilians and damage to infrastructure.

Some incidents, including a strike on a school, have triggered international condemnation and investigations.

7. International Reactions

Many countries and organizations have responded.

Important actors include:

the United Nations

the European Union

regional Middle Eastern governments

Some governments have called for ceasefires and negotiations, while others support military actions against Iran’s nuclear program.

8. Why the War Matters Globally

The conflict is considered extremely dangerous because:

It risks a large regional war across the Middle East.

It could disrupt global oil supplies.

It raises fears about nuclear proliferation.

It involves multiple powerful militaries and alliances.

Experts warn that escalation could involve more countries if the conflict spreads.

In essence:
The recent Iran war is a major geopolitical conflict in which the United States and Israel launched large-scale strikes on Iran—targeting nuclear and military capabilities—while Iran retaliates with missile and drone attacks, creating a rapidly escalating regional crisis.
